The Easy 80% — Rules Work
▸ Good matcher → both "John David Smith" and "JD Smith" resolve to Member #4821
▸ Association = GROUP BY on the Salesforce match ID
▸ Temporal window — findings within 90 days auto-cluster
▸ No AI required — a SQL query handles this
The Hard 20% — Where Complexity Lives
▸ No CRM match — unmatched findings that might still be related
▸ Dormant cases — 6 months silence, then a new article surfaces
▸ Cross-matter — one person, two separate conduct issues
▸ Oblique references — "the Melbourne tax agent case" without naming anyone
Approaches by Complexity Layer
Layer 1: Rules
GROUP BY sf_match_id within a time window. Handles all resolved matches trivially. No model, no cost, deterministic.
HANDLES ~80%
Layer 2: Clustering
BIRCH or HDBSCAN on embeddings of unmatched findings. Catches thematic groupings the matcher missed. Incremental, runs on CPU.
HANDLES ~15%
Layer 3: Human Review
Surface ambiguous cases to analysts. Correlation clustering incorporates their must-link/cannot-link feedback for future runs.
HANDLES ~5%
The real question isn't "should we use an LLM for association?" — it's "how good is our matcher?" If matching resolves identities reliably, association collapses to a GROUP BY. The complexity budget belongs upstream in extraction and matching, not here.
